Stephen King

The man who authored a generation's nightmares.

A lifetime spent honing his craft

  • 1947- Stephen King is born in Portland, Maine.
  • 1949- King's father, a merchant seaman, leaves his family. Stephen and his brohter are raised by his single mother.
  • 1965- King publishes his first short story, "I Was A Teenage Grave Robber," in Comics Review.
  • 1967- King publishes his first professional short story, "The Glass Floor," in Startling Mystery Stories.
  • 1970- King graduates from University of Maine with a Bachelor of Arts in English and certificate to teach high school
  • 1971- King marries Tabitha Spruce.
  • 1973- While working as a high school English teacher, King sells his first novel, "Carrie."
  • 1977- Stephen King publishes "The Shining." It becomes one of the landmark horror novels of the 20th century.
  • 1980s & 1990s- Stephen King becomes a perennially bestselling author. Many of his films are adapted into films, including "Cujo" and "Pet Semetary."
  • 1999- Stephen King is hit by a minivan while on a walk. He barely survives. His recovery last for months, during which he writes "Dreamcatcher."
  • 2014- Stephen King receives the National Medal of Arts from President Barack Obama.
